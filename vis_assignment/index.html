Big Five European Football Leagues (2021/2022 Season)

The Premier League, La Liga, Bundesliga, Serie A, Ligue 1 are among the most entertaining football leagues not only in Europe, but also the world. This visualisation explores these five European football leagues over the 2021/2022 season.

Player Nationalities

 Europe is the most represented continent in the five leagues, which is hardly surprising since these leagues are based in Europe. With 436 players, Spain is the leading nation in the world.

 Africa and South America are also very well represented in the five leagues, with Senegal and Brazil the leading nation in their respective continents.

Clinical or Wasteful

 Expected Goals (xG) measures the likelihood that a player scores from a particular situation during a game. A player who scores more goals than their total xG is overperforming in front of goal, whereas a player underperformed if their goals scored is lower than their xG.

 During the 2021/2022 season, Wissam Ben Yedder, Son Heung Min and Martin Terrier were 20-goal players who significantly outperformed their total xG, while Burak Yılmaz and Iñaki Williams were the two most underperforming players based on the xG conversion.

Insights on Player Ages

 During the 2021/2022 season, the most frequent player age group among the five leagues is the 24 to 26 age group.

 However, this is not the case for all leagues. For both Bundesliga and Ligue 1, the younger 21 to 23 age group is the most frequent age group. As for La Liga, the most frequent age group is older at 27 to 29.

Passing Statistics

 Considering players who played 20 games and above, the median passes attempted per player was the highest in Ligue 1. Marco Verrati attempted and completed the most passes per 90 minutes. Lionel Messi completed the most progressive passes per 90 minutes, which suggests that he was among the best players in moving his team forward with his ball distribution.

Ball Playing Goalkeepers

 Gone are the days where goalkeepers only stopped shots. In the 2021/2022 season, numbers show that first choice goalkeepers from higher ranked teams have a higher clean sheet rate, but they also performed passes at shorter distances on average.

 Unsurprisingly, a higher clean sheet rate translates to fewer goals conceded per 90 minutes in general.
