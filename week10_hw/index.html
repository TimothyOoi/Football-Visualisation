Visualisation on the Big 5 European Football Leagues (2021/2022 Season)

The Premier League, Spanish La Liga, Bundesliga, Serie A, Ligue 1 are among the most entertaining football leagues not only in Europe, but also the world. This visualisation explores these five European football leagues over the 2021/2022 season.

Player nationalities

Europe is the most represented continent in the five leagues, which is hardly surprising since these leagues are based in Europe. Africa and South America are also very well represented in the five leagues, with Africa boasting Premier League household names Mohamed Salah and Sadio Mane, while South America is home to superstars Lionel Messi and Neymar.

Clinical or simply lucky?

Expected Goals (xG) measures the likelihood that a player scores from a particular situation during a game. The value of xG ranges from zero to one, where zero represents a chance which is impossible to score. Conversely, a value of one means that a player is expected to score every single time. By analysing the xG and goals scored for a player, we can understand the shooting ability of luck of a particular player. A player who consistently scores more goals than their total xG probably has an above average shooting/finishing ability. Conversely, a player who has fewer goals scored compared to their total xG may be more wasteful in front goal, or simply subject to poor luck. During the 2021/2022 season, Wissam Ben Yedder, Son Heung Min and Martin Terrier were 20-goal players who significantly outperformed their total xG, while Burak Yılmaz and Iñaki Williams were the two most underperforming players based on the conversion of xG.
